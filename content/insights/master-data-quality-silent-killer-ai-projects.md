Item masters. Customer records. Vendor data.

This is where most AI projects quietly fail.

Not at launch.

In production.

## What Actually Happens

A model is trained.

An agent is deployed.

Outputs look promising.

Then:

- duplicate customers create conflicting results
- item descriptions don’t match across systems
- vendor data is incomplete or inconsistent

The system starts producing noise.

## Why It’s So Hard to Spot

Master data issues don’t fail loudly.

They fail gradually:

- small inconsistencies
- slight mismatches
- subtle errors in outputs

Individually, they look minor.

Collectively, they break trust.

## The Downstream Effect

When master data is broken:

- forecasting degrades
- reporting becomes inconsistent
- automation produces exceptions instead of efficiency
- teams revert to manual validation

AI doesn’t collapse.

It becomes unreliable.

## What Good Looks Like

Strong master data means:

- standardized structures
- consistent naming and classification
- defined ownership
- controlled creation and updates

It’s not about perfection.

It’s about consistency and control.

## Bottom Line

Every AI system inherits your master data.

If it’s clean, AI scales value.

If it’s broken, AI scales noise.
